About

Hi! I'm Beatriz :)
I’m a philosophy grad student based in Lisbon, working at the intersection of philosophy of mathematics, cognition, and AI.

Since publishing my first paper at 21 (literature is my second passion!) and later joining the same journal as a reviewer, I became especially invested in the art of clear argumentation, careful writing, and collaborative academic work.

On the Research page you'll find current projects and developing ideas that bridge technical work and conceptual analysis...

This is me when I got into uni! (About 5 years ago hehe)

My research explores the limits of computation in human and artificial minds, drawing on philosophy of mathematics, logic, and cognitive science. I’m especially interested in how formal systems model (and sometimes fail to model) abstraction, representation, and reasoning, and in what these limitations imply for ambitious claims about AI and cognitive architectures. Current projects grow out of this general picture and focus on computational and cognitive constraints in mathematical thinking, the role of abstraction in concept formation, and critical assessments of how contemporary AI systems are theorized within philosophy and cognitive science.

Recommendations

Some books I've been reading or recommending for people who want to think about logic, math, and minds.

Absolute Provability and Safe Knowledge of Axioms — Timothy Williamson

Honestly? A great paper...

An Introduction to Gödel's Theorems — Peter Smith

The clearest guide I know to Gödel's incompleteness theorems.

Psychosemantics — Jerry Fodor

An excellent launchpad to computation theories of mind.

Brain Bisection and The Unity of Consciousness — Thomas Nagel

Split-brain cases and what they tell us about the mind.
