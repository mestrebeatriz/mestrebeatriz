About Me

Hi! I’m Beatriz :) I’m a Philosophy Grad student based in Lisbon, working at the intersection of Philosophy of Mathematics, Cognition and AI. My research focuses mainly on the limits of computation (human or artificial) and on how formal theories of Logic and Mathematics can relate to real cognition.

Since publishing my first paper at 21 (Literature is my second passion!) and later joining the same journal as a reviewer, I became especially invested in the art of clear argumentation, careful writing and collaborative academic work: that’s why we do what we do. These experiences deeply influenced my current projects and my big-time goal of contributing to a more methodologically self-aware Cognitive Science.

On the Research page you will find current projects and developing ideas that bridge technical work and conceptual analysis. I also included a Reading Recommendations page, where I collect papers and books that have been important for my thinking and that I suggest to friends, students and colleagues. If any of this ever overlaps with your interests OR if you’d like to discuss collaborations, RA work or supervision, feel free to get in touch with me via de Contact page.

Research

My research explores the limits of computation in human and artificial minds, drawing on philosophy of mathematics, logic, and cognitive science. I’m especially interested in how formal systems model (and sometimes fail to model) abstraction, representation, and reasoning, and in what these limitations imply for ambitious claims about AI and cognitive architectures. Current projects focus on computational and cognitive constraints in mathematical thinking, the role of abstraction in concept formation, and critical assessments of how contemporary AI systems are theorized within philosophy and cognitive science.

Recommendations

Some books I've been reading or have read.

Gödel, Escher, Bach - an Eternal Golden Braid, by Douglas Hofstadter.

This is a relatively simple introduction to the works of some of the most brilliant mathematicians; a great gateway if you are looking to get into Mathematics or Metamathematics

An Introduction to Gödel's Theorems, by Peter Smith

If you want to get to know Gödel, this is the best place to start. It makes the concepts used in the proofs extremely accessible to the general public - a great introduction!
